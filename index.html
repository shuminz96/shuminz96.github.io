About

I am a third-year PhD student in STEM Education at the University of Nevada, Reno, where my research focuses on the intersection of generative AI and K-12 education — particularly how pre-service and in-service teachers adopt, adapt, and make sense of AI tools in their professional practice.

My dissertation applies Design-Based Research to understand how pre-service science teachers engage in AI-assisted lesson planning, examining the interplay of technological knowledge, pedagogical reasoning, and professional identity formation.

Before joining UNR, I taught General Technology at Xiamen No. 1 High School of Fujian Province — one of China's key provincial high schools — a formative experience that deeply informs my interest in teacher marginalization and non-core subject identities.

I hold an M.Ed. in Science and Technology Education from East China Normal University (Shanghai) and a B.S. in Educational Technology from Fujian Normal University. I am originally from Fuzhou, Fujian, China.

Research

01

Generative AI in K-12 Education

How teachers and students interact with large language models in classroom contexts, with emphasis on lesson planning, prompt engineering, and epistemic trust.

02

Teacher Technology Adoption & TPACK

Examining how pre-service and in-service teachers develop technological pedagogical content knowledge when integrating AI tools into science instruction.

03

Teacher Identity & Professional Marginalization

Qualitative and ethnographic inquiry into how non-core subject teachers — especially in China — construct professional identities within institutional constraints.

04

Chinese Teachers on Social Media

Ethnographic fieldwork on Xiaohongshu (小红书), studying how Chinese teachers discuss, share, and negotiate AI adoption in their professional communities online.

05

AR & LLM-based Pedagogical Agents

As part of the NSF RITEL project, investigating augmented reality and language-model agents as instructional supports in elementary science classrooms.

06

Design-Based Research

Methodological interest in iterative, participatory design cycles as a framework for studying and improving technology-integrated learning environments.
